Lesson: The Tower of Babel

After the flood, people began to repopulate the earth. They all spoke the same language and decided to build a city with a tall tower that reached the heavens. They wanted to make a name for themselves and avoid being scattered across the earth. However, this plan went against God’s desire for humanity to fill the earth and trust in Him. To stop their plans, God confused their language so they could no longer understand one another. This caused them to scatter across the earth, forming different nations and languages.

  • The Unity: After the flood, everyone spoke the same language and worked together.
  • The Tower: People decided to build a tall tower to make a name for themselves and avoid scattering.
  • God’s Intervention: God confused their language to stop their plans.
  • The Scattering: The people spread out across the earth, forming different nations.
  • God’s Plan: God desires unity through His purpose, not human pride.

Assessment Questions

Answer the questions based on your age group:

3-5 Years Old

  • What did the people build?
  • Why did they build the tower?
  • What did God do to stop them?
  • What happened to the people after God confused their language?

6-7 Years Old

  • Why did God confuse their language?
  • What does the story teach us about pride?
  • How did God’s plan bring people closer to Him?

8-10 Years Old

  • Why did the people want to build a tower to the heavens?
  • What does this story teach us about trusting God’s plans?
  • How can we seek unity in ways that honor God?

11-15 Years Old

  • How does the Tower of Babel reflect humanity’s struggle with pride?
  • What is the significance of God’s intervention in this story?
  • How does this story show the importance of following God’s plan?
  • How can we build unity based on God’s purpose in our lives today?
