Moses and the Burning Bush

"God Calls Us to His Purpose"

Lesson: God Calls Moses

While Moses was tending sheep in the wilderness, he saw an incredible sight: a bush that was on fire but not burning up. God spoke to Moses from the bush and told him to take off his sandals because he was standing on holy ground. God called Moses to lead His people out of slavery in Egypt. Even though Moses felt unworthy and afraid, God promised to be with him. This story reminds us that God calls us to fulfill His purpose, and He will equip us for the task.

  • The Encounter: Moses saw a burning bush that did not burn up.
  • God’s Call: God called Moses to lead the Israelites out of Egypt.
  • Holy Ground: Moses removed his sandals as a sign of respect for God’s presence.
  • Moses’ Doubts: Moses felt unworthy and unsure, but God reassured him.
  • God’s Promise: God promised to be with Moses and gave him signs to show His power.

Assessment Questions

Answer the questions based on your age group:

3-5 Years Old

  • What did Moses see in the wilderness?
  • Who spoke to Moses from the bush?
  • What did God tell Moses to do?
  • What did Moses take off to stand on holy ground?

6-7 Years Old

  • Why was the burning bush special?
  • What did God ask Moses to do?
  • How did God reassure Moses?

8-10 Years Old

  • Why did Moses feel unworthy to answer God’s call?
  • How did God show Moses that He would be with him?
  • What does this story teach us about following God’s purpose?

11-15 Years Old

  • What does the burning bush symbolize about God’s power and presence?
  • How did God prepare Moses for the task ahead?
  • What does this story teach us about overcoming fear when following God’s call?
  • How can we apply Moses’ obedience to our lives today?
