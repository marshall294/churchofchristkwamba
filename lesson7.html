"God Calls Us to His Purpose"

Lesson: Moses and the Burning Bush

Moses was tending sheep in the desert when he saw a bush that was on fire but didn’t burn up. As he approached, God called to him from the bush and gave him a special mission: to rescue the Israelites from slavery in Egypt. Moses felt afraid and unsure, but God promised to be with him. This story shows us that God has a purpose for each of us and that He will equip us for what He calls us to do.

  • The Encounter: God appears to Moses through a burning bush.

    Exodus 3:2-3: "There the angel of the Lord appeared to him in flames of fire from within a bush. Moses saw that though the bush was on fire it did not burn up. So Moses thought, 'I will go over and see this strange sight—why the bush does not burn up.'"

  • The Call: God calls Moses to deliver His people from Egypt.

    Exodus 3:10: "So now, go. I am sending you to Pharaoh to bring my people the Israelites out of Egypt."

  • The Doubt: Moses feels unworthy and afraid to accept God’s call.

    Exodus 3:11: "But Moses said to God, 'Who am I that I should go to Pharaoh and bring the Israelites out of Egypt?'"

  • The Assurance: God promises His presence and power.

    Exodus 3:12: "And God said, 'I will be with you. And this will be the sign to you that it is I who have sent you: When you have brought the people out of Egypt, you will worship God on this mountain.'"

Assessment Questions

3-5 Years Old

  • 1. What did Moses see in the desert?
  • 2. Who spoke to Moses from the bush?
  • 3. What special job did God give Moses?
  • 4. How did Moses feel when God called him?
  • 5. Who promised to help Moses?
  • 6. Why was the bush so amazing?
  • 7. Why is it important to listen to God?

6-7 Years Old

  • 1. What made the burning bush different from other bushes?
  • 2. What mission did God give to Moses?
  • 3. Why was Moses afraid?
  • 4. How did God encourage Moses?
  • 5. What does Moses’ story tell us about trusting God?
  • 6. How do we know God is with us when He gives us a mission?
  • 7. Why does God call people to do His work?

8-10 Years Old

  • 1. How did Moses react when he saw the burning bush?
  • 2. Why was Moses hesitant to accept God’s call?
  • 3. What does this story teach us about God's presence?
  • 4. How does God prepare us for the tasks He gives us?
  • 5. What should we do when we feel afraid to follow God's plan?
  • 6. How did God show His power through Moses later?
  • 7. How can we trust God with our purpose today?

11-15 Years Old

  • 1. What qualities did God see in Moses even when Moses doubted himself?
  • 2. How does Moses' encounter with the burning bush show God's holiness?
  • 3. Why is it important to trust God's calling even when we feel weak?
  • 4. What excuses did Moses give to God, and how did God respond?
  • 5. How does this story teach us about God's patience and assurance?
  • 6. How can we apply Moses’ story to our own calling and purpose?
  • 7. What does Exodus 3 teach us about God's character?
