Creation Story (Genesis 1–2)

"God Made Everything"

Lesson: The Creation Story

In the beginning, God created everything. Over six days, God made the heavens and the earth, light and dark, water and sky, plants, animals, and people. On the seventh day, God rested.

  • Day 1: God created light and separated day from night.
  • Day 2: God created the sky.
  • Day 3: God made land, seas, plants, and trees.
  • Day 4: God made the sun, moon, and stars.
  • Day 5: God created birds and sea creatures.
  • Day 6: God made land animals and humans.
  • Day 7: God rested and blessed the day.

Assessment Questions

Answer the questions based on your age group:

3-5 Years Old

  • Who made the world?
  • What did God create on the first day?
  • What did God make on the fifth day?
  • What did God do on the seventh day?
  • Who did God create in His image?

6-7 Years Old

  • What is the first thing God created?
  • What separates day and night?
  • Name two things God made on the third day.
  • What are the lights in the sky that God made on the fourth day?
  • Why did God rest on the seventh day?

8-10 Years Old

  • What does it mean that humans are made in God’s image?
  • What did God say after He finished each day of creation?
  • Why do you think God created plants and animals before humans?
  • What is the purpose of the sun, moon, and stars?
  • How can we rest like God did on the seventh day?

11-15 Years Old

  • What does the Creation story teach us about God’s power and creativity?
  • How does knowing you are created in God’s image affect the way you see yourself?
  • What can we learn from God resting on the seventh day?
  • How does the Creation story connect to taking care of the environment today?
  • Why is the Creation story important to our faith?
