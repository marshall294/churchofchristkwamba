Lesson: David and Goliath

In 1 Samuel 17, we read about David, a young shepherd boy, who faced the giant Goliath with nothing but a sling, five stones, and faith in God. While the Israelite army trembled in fear, David trusted God’s strength and declared that the battle belonged to the Lord. With one stone, David defeated Goliath, showing us that with God on our side, we can overcome the “giants” in our lives — no matter how big they seem.

  • Faith in God, Not in Size: David was smaller and weaker than Goliath, but he trusted God’s power, not weapons or armor.
    1 Samuel 17:45 — “David said to the Philistine, ‘You come against me with sword and spear, but I come against you in the name of the Lord Almighty…’”
    Zechariah 4:6 — “‘Not by might nor by power, but by my Spirit,’ says the Lord Almighty.”
  • Courage Over Fear: While the Israelite soldiers were afraid, David showed courage because he knew God was with him.
    1 Samuel 17:32 — “David said to Saul, ‘Let no one lose heart on account of this Philistine; your servant will go and fight him.’”
    Joshua 1:9 — “Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid… for the Lord your God will be with you wherever you go.”
  • God Uses the Ordinary: David defeated Goliath with just a sling and a stone, proving that God can use simple things and ordinary people for extraordinary victories.
    1 Samuel 17:50 — “So David triumphed over the Philistine with a sling and a stone.”
    1 Corinthians 1:27 — “God chose the weak things of the world to shame the strong.”
  • The Battle Belongs to the Lord: David reminded everyone that the victory was God’s, not man’s.
    1 Samuel 17:47 — “…for the battle is the Lord’s, and he will give all of you into our hands.”
    Romans 8:31 — “If God is for us, who can be against us?”

Assessment Questions

3-5 Years Old

  • 1. Who was the big man that fought Israel?
  • 2. Who was the young boy that trusted God?
  • 3. What did David use to fight Goliath?
  • 4. Who helped David win?
  • 5. Can God help us when we face big problems?

6-7 Years Old

  • 1. Why was Goliath scary to the Israelites?
  • 2. Why wasn’t David afraid?
  • 3. What did David say about the battle?
  • 4. How did David defeat Goliath?
  • 5. What can we learn about trusting God from this story?

8-10 Years Old

  • 1. What made David different from the Israelite soldiers?
  • 2. Why did Saul doubt David could fight Goliath?
  • 3. What lesson does David’s faith teach us about courage?
  • 4. Why was it important that David gave glory to God after the battle?
  • 5. What are some “giants” we face in life today, and how can God help us?

11-15 Years Old

  • 1. How does David’s victory show that God uses the weak to shame the strong?
  • 2. Why was David confident when everyone else was afraid?
  • 3. What role did faith play in David’s victory?
  • 4. How can we apply the principle “the battle is the Lord’s” in our lives today?
  • 5. What personal “giants” do you face, and how can God help you overcome them?
