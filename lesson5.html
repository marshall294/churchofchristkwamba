Lesson: Abraham’s Faith in God’s Covenant

God called Abraham to leave his home and travel to a land He would show him. God promised Abraham that He would make him the father of many nations and bless all the families of the earth through him. Even though Abraham and his wife Sarah were old and had no children, Abraham trusted God. God made a covenant with Abraham, giving him the sign of circumcision and promising him countless descendants. Abraham’s faith in God’s promises inspires us to trust in God’s plans, even when they seem impossible.

  • The Call: God called Abraham to leave his home and follow Him.
  • The Promise: God promised Abraham that he would be the father of many nations.
  • The Covenant: God made a covenant with Abraham and gave him the sign of circumcision.
  • The Test of Faith: Abraham trusted God, even though he and Sarah were old and childless.
  • The Fulfillment: God blessed Abraham with a son, Isaac, and fulfilled His promises.

Assessment Questions

Answer the questions based on your age group:

3-5 Years Old

  • What did God ask Abraham to do?
  • What did God promise Abraham?
  • What did Abraham trust God to do?
  • What did God give Abraham to show His promise?

6-7 Years Old

  • Why did God call Abraham to leave his home?
  • What was the sign of God’s covenant with Abraham?
  • How did God show He keeps His promises to Abraham?

8-10 Years Old

  • What does Abraham’s faith teach us about trusting God?
  • How did God’s covenant with Abraham show His faithfulness?
  • What can we learn about God’s promises through this story?

11-15 Years Old

  • Why is Abraham’s faith important in understanding God’s covenant?
  • How does this story show God’s ability to do the impossible?
  • What does this story teach us about being faithful to God’s calling?
  • How does the story of Abraham inspire us to trust God in uncertain times?
