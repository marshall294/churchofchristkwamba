Abraham’s Promise

"Faith in God's Covenant"

Lesson: Abraham’s Promise (Genesis 12–17)

God made a covenant with Abraham, promising to bless him and make his descendants a great nation. Despite challenges, Abraham trusted God's promises, showing great faith. Through Abraham, God began the plan of salvation for the world. In Genesis 12-17, we see God's call to Abraham, the covenant of circumcision, and the promise of a child in Abraham's old age, which all demonstrate God's faithfulness to His word.

  • God's Call: God called Abraham to leave his country and promised to make him the father of a great nation.
    • Genesis 12:1-2 - "The Lord had said to Abram, 'Go from your country, your people and your father’s household to the land I will show you. I will make you into a great nation, and I will bless you; I will make your name great, and you will be a blessing.'"
    • Explanation: Abraham was called to trust in God's promises and step out in faith, leaving everything familiar behind.
  • The Promise of Descendants: God promises Abraham numerous descendants, even though he is old and childless.
    • Genesis 15:5 - "He took him outside and said, 'Look up at the sky and count the stars—if indeed you can count them.' Then he said to him, 'So shall your offspring be.'"
    • Explanation: God reassured Abraham that his descendants would be countless, symbolized by the stars in the sky.
  • Covenant of Circumcision: God establishes a covenant with Abraham and requires circumcision as a sign of this covenant.
    • Genesis 17:10-11 - "This is my covenant with you and your descendants after you, the covenant you are to keep: Every male among you shall be circumcised. You are to undergo circumcision, and it will be the sign of the covenant between me and you."
    • Explanation: Circumcision is a physical symbol of Abraham’s faith and obedience to God's covenant.
  • The Promise of Isaac: Despite being 100 years old, God promises Abraham that he will have a son named Isaac.
    • Genesis 17:19 - "Then God said, 'Yes, but your wife Sarah will bear you a son, and you will call him Isaac. I will establish my covenant with him as an everlasting covenant for his descendants after him.'"
    • Explanation: God fulfills His promise by giving Abraham and Sarah a son, Isaac, through whom God's covenant will continue.

Assessment Questions

3-5 Years Old

  • 1. Who did God speak to in the story?
  • 2. What promise did God make to Abraham?
  • 3. What did God ask Abraham to do?
  • 4. How old was Abraham when he had a son?
  • 5. What was the name of Abraham’s son?
  • 6. What did God say Abraham’s family would be like?
  • 7. Why did Abraham trust God?

6-7 Years Old

  • 1. What did God promise Abraham?
  • 2. How did God show His faithfulness to Abraham?
  • 3. What was the sign of God’s covenant with Abraham?
  • 4. Why did Abraham and Sarah laugh when they heard they would have a son?
  • 5. How many descendants did God promise Abraham?
  • 6. Where did God ask Abraham to go?
  • 7. What can we learn from Abraham’s trust in God?

8-10 Years Old

  • 1. What does the covenant with Abraham teach us about God’s promises?
  • 2. How did Abraham’s faith affect his relationship with God?
  • 3. How is the covenant of circumcision significant for Abraham and his descendants?
  • 4. How can we trust God’s promises in our own lives?
  • 5. What does it mean to be obedient to God’s commands like Abraham?
  • 6. How did God fulfill His promise to Abraham?
  • 7. How does Abraham’s story relate to our faith today?

11-15 Years Old

  • 1. What was the significance of God’s covenant with Abraham?
  • 2. How did Abraham’s obedience reflect his faith in God’s promises?
  • 3. How does the story of Abraham help us understand God’s plan for salvation?
  • 4. How is Abraham’s story related to Jesus Christ and His coming?
  • 5. In what ways can we apply Abraham’s faith and trust in God to our own lives?
  • 6. What role did Isaac play in God’s covenant with Abraham?
  • 7. How can we trust God even when we don’t understand His plan for us?
