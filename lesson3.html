Lesson: God’s Promise to Noah

In the time of Noah, the world was full of wickedness. God saw that Noah was a righteous man, so He decided to save Noah and his family from a great flood that would cover the earth. God gave Noah specific instructions to build an ark. Noah obeyed God, and when the flood came, Noah, his family, and the animals were safe in the ark. After the flood, God made a promise to Noah that He would never again destroy the earth with a flood, and He placed a rainbow in the sky as a sign of this promise.

  • God’s Call: God saw that Noah was righteous, so He told Noah to build an ark to save his family and animals.
  • The Ark: Noah obeyed God and built a massive ark, as instructed by God.
  • The Flood: Noah, his family, and the animals entered the ark, and the floodwaters came.
  • God’s Promise: After the flood, God made a covenant with Noah, promising never to destroy the earth with a flood again.
  • The Rainbow: God placed a rainbow in the sky as a sign of His promise to Noah.

Assessment Questions

Answer the questions based on your age group:

3-5 Years Old

  • Who built the ark?
  • What did God tell Noah to do?
  • What did Noah put on the ark?
  • What did God send after the rain?
  • What did the rainbow mean?

6-7 Years Old

  • Why did Noah build the ark?
  • Who went into the ark with Noah?
  • What was the sign of God’s promise to Noah?
  • How long did the flood last?
  • What did God say He would never do again?

8-10 Years Old

  • How did Noah show his faith in God?
  • What did God do to show His faithfulness to Noah after the flood?
  • What can we learn from Noah’s obedience to God?
  • What is the significance of the rainbow in the story of Noah’s Ark?
  • How can we trust God’s promises today?

11-15 Years Old

  • Why was Noah chosen to survive the flood?
  • What does Noah’s obedience teach us about trusting God in difficult times?
  • How does the rainbow symbolize God’s faithfulness?
  • How can we apply God’s promise to never destroy the earth with a flood in our lives today?
  • Why is it important to keep God’s promises in mind as we face challenges?
