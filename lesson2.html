Lesson: Adam and Eve's Disobedience

In the Garden of Eden, God created the first man and woman, Adam and Eve. They had everything they needed, but God gave them one command: not to eat from the tree of knowledge of good and evil. Unfortunately, they were tempted by the serpent (Satan) and disobeyed God's command. As a result, sin entered the world, and they were separated from God.

  • God’s Creation: God created Adam and Eve and placed them in the beautiful Garden of Eden.
  • The Command: God told them not to eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil.
  • The Temptation: Satan, in the form of a serpent, tempted Eve to eat the fruit.
  • Disobedience: Adam and Eve ate the fruit and disobeyed God.
  • Consequences: They were ashamed, and God cast them out of the garden.
  • God’s Promise: Even in their disobedience, God promised a Savior who would defeat Satan.

Assessment Questions

Answer the questions based on your age group:

3-5 Years Old

  • Who made Adam and Eve?
  • What did God tell Adam and Eve not to do?
  • What animal tricked Eve into eating the fruit?
  • What happened when Adam and Eve ate the fruit?
  • Did Adam and Eve listen to God’s command?

6-7 Years Old

  • What did God create for Adam and Eve to live in?
  • What did the serpent say to Eve in the garden?
  • Why did Adam and Eve eat the fruit?
  • What were the consequences of Adam and Eve’s disobedience?
  • What did God promise after Adam and Eve disobeyed?

8-10 Years Old

  • What can we learn from Adam and Eve’s disobedience to God?
  • How does sin affect our relationship with God?
  • Why is it important to obey God’s commands?
  • How did Satan tempt Eve in the garden?
  • What is the significance of God’s promise for a Savior?

11-15 Years Old

  • How can we guard ourselves against temptation like Eve?
  • Why did God give Adam and Eve the command not to eat from the tree?
  • What do you think the consequences of disobedience are in our lives today?
  • How can we restore our relationship with God after we sin?
  • How does the story of Adam and Eve point to the need for Jesus as the Savior?
